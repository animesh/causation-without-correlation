REAL BIOLOGICAL EXAMPLES: WHERE BOTH UP AND DOWN ACTIVATE GENES

QUESTION: Is there a real biological example where expression going up OR down

#1 OSMOTIC STRESS (Very Common - All Organisms)
───────────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT HAPPENS:
  Cell detects water/salt balance deviation
  Response is SAME whether osmolarity goes UP or DOWN
  
MECHANISM:
  High salt (300 → 350 mOsm):
    ← Activate GPD1, STL1, AQY2
    ← Produce osmolytes
    ← Reduce water loss
  
  Low salt (300 → 250 mOsm):
    ← Activate GPD1, STL1, AQY2  ← SAME genes!
    ← Strengthen cell walls
    ← Prepare for water influx

GENE EXAMPLE - GPD1:
  Glycerol-3-phosphate dehydrogenase
  Activated by BOTH high AND low osmolarity
  Used to produce glycerol (osmolyte)
  Protects against water loss AND gain

CORRELATION:
  Osmolarity vs GPD1 expression: ρ ≈ 0
  (Because up and down osmolarity both activate it)

CAUSATION:
  Clear! Osmolarity changes → GPD1 activation

EQUATION:
  GPD1 expression ∝ |osmolarity - 300|


#2 CALCIUM SIGNALING (Universal in Neurons)
─────────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT HAPPENS:
  Ca²⁺ oscillates in cells
  Downstream genes respond to CHANGE in [Ca²⁺]
  (Not direction, but magnitude of change)

MECHANISM:
  Ca²⁺ INCREASES (100 → 500 nM):
    ← Calmodulin-Ca²⁺ forms
    ← Activates calcineurin
    ← Dephosphorylates TFEB, MEF2, etc.
    ← Gene expression changes

  Ca²⁺ DECREASES (500 → 100 nM):
    ← Dissociation triggers conformational change
    ← ALSO activates calcineurin briefly!
    ← Different mechanism, similar output
    ← Different genes activated (depression vs potentiation)

GENE EXAMPLE - CREB (in neurons):
  Phosphorylated during Ca²⁺ spikes (up)
  Dephosphorylated during Ca²⁺ drops (down)
  Both affect downstream gene expression
  Critical for memory formation (LTP and LTD)

CORRELATION:
  [Ca²⁺] vs CREB phosphorylation: ρ ≈ 0
  (Both increase and decrease cause changes)

CAUSATION:
  Clear! Ca²⁺ changes → CREB changes → gene expression

EQUATION:
  Gene expression ∝ f(|Δ[Ca²⁺]|)  where f depends on direction


#3 HEAT SHOCK PROTEINS (Universal!)
──────────────────────────────────

WHAT HAPPENS:
  Cells respond to temperature stress
  Response is SAME whether temp goes UP or DOWN

MECHANISM:
  Temperature INCREASES (37 → 42°C):
    ← Proteins denature
    ← Activate HSP70, HSP90
    ← Refold proteins
    ← Restore folding

  Temperature DECREASES (37 → 25°C):
    ← Cold shock proteins (different HSPs!)
    ← Prevent protein aggregation
    ← ALSO activate heat shock pathway
    ← Different genes, similar magnitude

GENE EXAMPLE - HSP90:
  Molecular chaperone
  Activated by heat AND cold
  Also activated by:
    • Chemical stress
    • Oxidative stress
    • Proteotoxic stress

CORRELATION:
  Temperature vs HSP90: ρ ≈ 0
  (Both extreme high and low activate it)

CAUSATION:
  Clear! Thermal stress → HSP activation

EQUATION:
  HSP expression ∝ |temperature - 37°C|


#4 pH STRESS (Bacteria, Yeast, Plants)
─────────────────────────────────────

WHAT HAPPENS:
  Cells maintain pH ≈ 7
  Deviations in EITHER direction trigger response

MECHANISM:
  pH INCREASES (7 → 7.5, basic):
    ← Activate acid-producing genes
    ← Increase H⁺ ATPase
    ← Restore to pH 7

  pH DECREASES (7 → 6.5, acidic):
    ← Activate base-producing genes
    ← Decrease H⁺ ATPase
    ← Restore to pH 7

GENE EXAMPLE - GRE2 (yeast):
  Glyoxalase II
  Activated by BOTH oxidative AND reducing stress
  Protects against reactive oxygen species
  Response depends on redox imbalance magnitude

CORRELATION:
  pH vs stress genes: ρ ≈ 0
  (Both high and low pH activate response)

CAUSATION:
  Clear! pH deviation → stress response

EQUATION:
  Stress gene expression ∝ |pH - 7|


#5 NUTRIENT LIMITATION (Most Relevant for Pao et al.!)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT HAPPENS:
  During yeast metabolic cycle
  Glucose oscillates
  Cells respond to MAGNITUDE of glucose change
  (This is EXACTLY what Pao et al. study!)

MECHANISM:
  GLUCOSE INCREASES:
    ← Growth mode activated
    ← CLN3, SWI4 rising
    ← Prepare for S-phase entry
    ← WHI5 rises to REPRESS SWI4

  GLUCOSE DECREASES:
    ← Survival mode activated
    ← Different genes activated
    ← Different WHI5 dynamics
    ← But causation relationship still there

WHI5 vs SWI4:
  Pao et al. show:
    Correlation(WHI5, SWI4) ≈ 0.35 (OUR DATA) or <0.1 (PAPER)
    But WHI5 clearly CAUSES SWI4 changes!
    
    WHI5 is a repressor, so:
      • When SWI4 wants to rise → WHI5 pushes back hard
      • When SWI4 should stay low → WHI5 effect weaker
      • Overall: low correlation, high causation

CAUSATION:
  Clear! WHI5 controls SWI4 checkpoint function

EQUATION:
  SWI4 dynamics ∝ f(WHI5 level, cell cycle phase)
  → Nonlinear and state-dependent


#6 GROWTH FACTOR SIGNALING (Animal Cells)
──────────────────────────────────────────

WHAT HAPPENS:
  Growth factor presence vs absence
  Both conditions activate different response programs

MECHANISM:
  GROWTH FACTOR PRESENT (e.g., EGF):
    ← Cell survival pathways on
    ← Division genes activated
    ← Growth mode

  GROWTH FACTOR ABSENT (withdrawal):
    ← Apoptosis genes activated
    ← Stress response genes on
    ← Survival mode OR death

CAUSATION:
  Clear! Growth factor controls major life/death decision
  But correlation might be low (different genes activated)

  (stimulus clearly controls genes)
